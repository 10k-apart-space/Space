SPACE

A responsive mobile up exploration of our solar system.

Each planet's size and distance from the sun is roughly scaled to their actual measurements based on current scientific knowledge. As you scroll towards the sun you'll learn an interesting fact about each planet.

Neptune

  • 49,244 km diameter / 60,190 day orbit
  • Neptune has 14 moons.

Uranus

  • 50,724 km diameter / 30,687 day orbit
  • Uranus hits the coldest temperatures of any planet.

Saturn

  • 116,464 km diameter / 10,756 day orbit
  • Saturn has the most extensive rings in the solar system.

Jupiter

  • 139,822 km diameter / 4,333 day orbit
  • Eight spacecraft have visited Jupiter.

Mars

  • 6,779 km diameter / 687 day orbit
  • Mars is home to the tallest mountain in the solar system.

Earth

  • 12,742 km diameter / 365.26 day orbit
  • Earth is the only planet not named after a god.

Venus

  • 12,104 km diameter / 225 day orbit
  • Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most other planets.

Mercury

  • 4,879 km diameter / 88 day orbit
  • Mercury does not have any moons or rings.
